How both products work — two layers
Layer 1 · Asset standard
How this asset always operates
Set once. Applies to everyone — owner, charter guest, rental guest, anyone. Never changes per visitor. This is the baseline the asset is always held to.
Marine: Aesop soap, Frette linens, crew uniform, service style
Property: housekeeping standard, welcome setup, systems at arrival temp
Layer 2 · Person profile
What's unique to this specific person
Activates when someone is arriving. Layers on top of the asset standard automatically. Generates the arrival checklist. Repeats for every visit — no re-entering preferences.
Owner: espresso at 6:45, gluten-free, firm pillows
Charter guest: shellfish allergy, champagne preference — saved for next time
Operations run continuously underneath both layers. A work order logged by the chief engineer doesn't care who's aboard. The maintenance schedule doesn't pause for a charter. Operations are always on — the asset standard and person profile sit on top of them.

The most common flow. Happens 10x more than anything else. A crew member or staff finds an issue and needs to log it. Three steps. Same every time. Doesn't matter if the owner is aboard, a charter is running, or the vessel is in a yard.
Three steps — always the same
1
Open Frame
See your tasks and any open alerts. What needs attention today — right now, before doing anything else.
2
Log the issue
Title, photo, which asset, assign to someone. Ten seconds. No forms, no email threads, no calls.
3
Back to work
Ticket is in the system. Captain or estate manager sees it instantly. Asset history updated. Done.
